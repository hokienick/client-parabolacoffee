# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

San Diego locals, late 20s to early 40s. They know their coffee. They support local businesses. They're regulars — people who become friends with the barista. Context: scrolling their phone on a sunny San Diego morning deciding where to get coffee, or looking us up after a friend recommended us.

## Product Purpose

Drive foot traffic to 3504 Adams Ave, Normal Heights. Secondarily: sell coffee roasts and merch online. The site should feel like the shop — warm, colorful, alive, and welcoming. It should make someone want to come in today.

## Brand Personality

Fun, friendly, and caring (their own words). Three words: **vibrant, communal, handcrafted**.

Voice: warm and enthusiastic without being performative. Real, not polished. Like a barista who genuinely loves what they do and loves meeting people.

## Anti-references

- Dark "premium roaster" aesthetic (Vercel-dark, moody black with amber — that's not who we are)
- Corporate coffee (Starbucks clean, Blue Bottle minimalism)
- Generic AI landing page (fade-in cards, muted palette, concentric circles as "design")
- Anything that feels like it was made for a VC-backed startup

## Design Principles

1. **The logo IS the brand** — the handwritten Parabola script is the most distinctive asset. Feature it, don't bury it.
2. **Community shows up in color** — the real site hero has 6+ vivid colors. Embrace the full palette.
3. **Imagery is non-negotiable** — this is a coffee shop. No colored rectangles where photos belong.
4. **Daytime energy** — this shop is open 7:30am–3pm only. The site should feel like morning sunlight, not a nightclub.
5. **Latin American roots** — the menu (Horchata Cold Brew, Café de Olla, Cardamom Latte, Vietnamese) tells a story. So should the site.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. All text meets contrast requirements. Images have meaningful alt text.
